Popular Destinations

Jaipur, Rajasthan

Known as the "Pink City," Jaipur offers majestic palaces, vibrant markets, and a glimpse into the royal history of Rajasthan.

Goa

Famous for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and Portuguese heritage, Goa is a popular tourist destination in India.

Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

Varanasi, situated on the banks of the sacred Ganges River, is a spiritual hub known for its ancient temples and religious ceremonies.

Munnar, Kerala

Munnar is a hill station in Kerala, blessed with lush tea plantations, breathtaking landscapes, and pleasant weather.

Ooty, Tamil Nadu

Located in the Nilgiri Hills, Ooty is a popular hill station known for its cool climate, picturesque landscapes, and tea gardens.

Coorg, Karnataka

Coorg, also known as Kodagu, is a scenic hill station famous for its coffee plantations, waterfalls, and trekking trails.

Madurai, Tamil Nadu

Madurai is a city known for its rich cultural heritage and famous Meenakshi Amman Temple, showcasing impressive Dravidian architecture.

Hampi, Karnataka

Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ancient ruins, stunning temples, and grandiose architecture from the Vijayanagara Empire.

Kodaikanal, Tamil Nadu

Kodaikanal, often referred to as the "Princess of Hill Stations," offers scenic beauty, tranquil lakes, and panoramic views of the Western Ghats.

Mysore, Karnataka

Mysore is renowned for its royal heritage, including the majestic Mysore Palace, vibrant Dasara festival, and intricate silk sarees.

Pondicherry

Pondicherry, also known as Puducherry, is a charming coastal town with French influence, beautiful beaches, and spiritual retreats.

Agra, Uttar Pradesh

Agra is home to the iconic Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World, attracting millions of visitors with its stunning architecture.

Amritsar, Punjab

Amritsar is known for its sacred Golden Temple, a spiritual and cultural center for Sikhs, along with its vibrant street food and historical sites.

Leh-Ladakh, Jammu and Kashmir

Leh-Ladakh offers breathtaking landscapes, serene monasteries, and thrilling adventures amidst the snow-capped Himalayan mountains.

Udaipur, Rajasthan

Udaipur, also known as the "City of Lakes," is famous for its beautiful palaces, romantic lakes, and vibrant Rajasthani culture.

Shimla, Himachal Pradesh

Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, is a popular hill station with colonial charm, scenic views, and adventure activities.

Darjeeling, West Bengal

Darjeeling is renowned for its tea plantations, toy train rides, stunning views of the Kanchenjunga mountain range, and Buddhist monasteries.

Rishikesh, Uttarakhand

Rishikesh is a spiritual town on the banks of the Ganges River, known for yoga and meditation retreats, adventure sports, and ancient temples.

Kochi, Kerala

Kochi, also known as Cochin, is a vibrant port city with a rich colonial history, enchanting backwaters, and a bustling spice market.

Khajuraho, Madhya Pradesh

Khajuraho is famous for its intricately carved temples depicting various forms of love, showcasing India's rich architectural heritage.

Ajanta and Ellora Caves, Maharashtra

The Ajanta and Ellora Caves are UNESCO World Heritage Sites, featuring ancient rock-cut caves adorned with exquisite sculptures and paintings.
